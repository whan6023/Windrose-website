TopCo Governance Requirement — Article 13 Board Approval

Windrose Holdings Limited (BE 1017.756.464) · HCIC VIII Acquisition Corp. · Draft · Not for circulation · 29 June 2026
v0.11 Structure — One Requirement, No Dissolution
TopCo (Windrose Holdings Limited, Belgian NV) stays alive. It remains as the controlling parent shareholder of Windrose Technology Inc. (WTI / the Company) following the Nasdaq listing. The only governance action required under the statuten is a single board resolution approving the listing under Article 13. No shareholder vote, no EGM, no Investor Consent, no dissolution.
1
Board Approval — Majority Preferred Directors Art. 13 statuten Only Required Step

Article 13 of the statuten requires that the board of directors of TopCo — including the Majority Preferred Directors appointed by holders of more than 60% of Preferred Share voting power — affirmatively approve the following matters before the Company lists on Nasdaq:

  1. iThe listing of the Company (WTI) on the Nasdaq Stock Market, including the dual-class share structure (Company Class A Common Stock, 20 votes/share, held by TopCo; Company Class B Common Stock, 1 vote/share, issued to HCIC shareholders)
  2. iiThe valuation of the Company implied by the BCA — being the Pre-Money Equity Value ($10.00 per share × ~138,565,537 aggregate Company shares ≈ $1.385 billion)
  3. iiiThe timing of the Company's Nasdaq listing and the consummation of the Transactions

The approval is documented as a certified copy of the relevant board resolution(s) of TopCo (the "TopCo Approval") and must be delivered to HCIC in form and substance reasonably acceptable to HCIC, no later than 30 days before the anticipated Closing Date and, in any event, prior to the filing of the Registration Statement on Form S-4 with the SEC.

Majority Preferred Directors. Under the statuten, the Majority Preferred Holders (holders of more than 60% of the combined voting power of all Preferred Shares — Series A-1, A-2, B-1, B-2, and C-1) are entitled to appoint a specified number of directors to the TopCo board. Their affirmative vote on the board is required by Article 13 for the listing approval. Identifying the current Majority Preferred Directors and confirming the approval process with Belgian counsel is the critical-path action item.

TopCo is a party to the BCA for the limited purpose of Section 4.13 (TopCo Board Approval) and related closing conditions. No joinder to the BCA is required for any other purpose because TopCo is not dissolving and is not a merging entity.

2
Actions Not Required in v0.11

Because TopCo remains alive and does not dissolve, the following actions that were required under the earlier v0.10 "Pre-Closing Restructuring" structure are not required in v0.11:

Removed — No Longer Required
  • Investor Consent from Majority Preferred Holders (Art. 27) — no TopCo dissolution is occurring
  • Waiver of "Deemed Liquidation Event" treatment (Art. 34) — the Transactions do not constitute a liquidation event; TopCo preferred shareholders retain all rights under the statuten
  • Waiver of liquidation preference waterfall — no liquidation; preferred shareholders keep their preferences in TopCo
  • Preferred Share Conversion (Art. 10(iii)) — TopCo preferred shares are not being converted; they remain in TopCo
  • Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM) — dissolution vote not required (Arts. 24, 27, 36)
  • Liquidator appointment and in-kind distribution authorisation (Art. 33)
  • Belgian Official Gazette publication and creditor protection period
  • KBO / BCE deregistration — TopCo does not cease to exist
  • Exhibit F (Form of TopCo Investor Consent) — removed from Transaction Documents

No Deemed Liquidation Event. Article 34 of the statuten is not triggered. The Transactions do not constitute a dissolution, winding-up, or sale of substantially all assets of TopCo. TopCo's preferred shareholders retain all liquidation preferences, anti-dilution rights, and contractual consent rights under the statuten following the Effective Time. No waivers are required from any preferred shareholder.

3
Drag-Along Rights — Automatic Termination Art. 11 statuten

Article 11 of the statuten provides that the drag-along rights set out therein terminate automatically upon the completion of a Qualifying IPO — defined as an initial public offering of any Group Company on a recognised securities exchange. The WTI Nasdaq listing is such a Qualifying IPO.

The consequences are:

  1. aDrag-along rights cannot be exercised by Wen Han (as the founding Class A shareholder of TopCo) to compel preferred shareholders to participate in the Transactions. This mechanism is therefore not available as a tool to ensure participation.
  2. bDrag-along rights terminate at Closing (the Effective Time). After the WTI Nasdaq listing is complete, no shareholder of TopCo can use the drag-along mechanism to compel other shareholders to sell or transfer their TopCo shares.
  3. cNo drag-along is needed in v0.11. Because TopCo is not dissolving and its shareholders are not receiving WTI shares in exchange for their TopCo shares, there is no "drag-along scenario" to navigate. Preferred shareholders remain shareholders of TopCo; they do not need to consent to any transfer of their own shares.

Wen Han's voting position. As the sole holder of Class A shares (3 votes each), Wen Han controls approximately 41.9% of TopCo's total voting power on a fully diluted basis. This is below the 50% threshold required to exercise drag-along rights under Article 11, which is an additional reason why the drag-along is not available as a mechanism to compel preferred holders — even if it had not terminated upon the Qualifying IPO.

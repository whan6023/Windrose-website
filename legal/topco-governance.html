TopCo Governance Requirement — Article 13 Board Approval

Windrose Holdings Limited (BE 1017.756.464) · HCIC VIII Acquisition Corp. · Draft · Not for circulation · 29 June 2026
v0.13 Structure — One Requirement, No Dissolution, Founder Holds Class A Directly, NYSE
TopCo (Windrose Holdings Limited, Belgian NV) stays alive. Following the NYSE listing, TopCo holds Company Class B Common Stock (1 vote/share) and remains the largest economic shareholder of WTI. Wen Han (Founder) holds Company Class A Common Stock (20 votes/share) directly as an individual — the super-voting founder class. The only governance action required under the statuten is a single board resolution under Article 13, which must now also cover the Founder Share Issuance and TopCo's share reclassification. No EGM, no Investor Consent, no dissolution, no Deemed Liquidation Event.
1
Board Approval — Majority Preferred Directors Art. 13 statuten Only Required Step

Article 13 of the statuten requires that the board of directors of TopCo — including the Majority Preferred Directors appointed by holders of more than 60% of Preferred Share voting power — affirmatively approve the following matters before the Company lists on the NYSE:

  1. iThe listing of the Company (WTI) on the New York Stock Exchange (NYSE), including the dual-class share structure (Company Class A Common Stock, 20 votes/share, held by Wen Han as Founder; Company Class B Common Stock, 1 vote/share, held by TopCo and issued to HCIC shareholders)
  2. iiThe valuation of the Company implied by the BCA — being the Pre-Money Equity Value ($10.00 per share × ~138,565,537 aggregate Company shares ≈ $1.385 billion)
  3. iiiThe timing of the Company's NYSE listing and the consummation of the Transactions
  4. ivThe Founder Share Issuance — the direct issuance of [●] shares of Company Class A Common Stock to Wen Han personally — and the reclassification of TopCo's existing WTI shares as Company Class B Common Stock (1 vote/share), including the resulting change in TopCo's voting position from Class A (20 votes/share) to Class B (1 vote/share)

Item (iv) is from v0.12, simplified in v0.13. The TopCo board (including Majority Preferred Directors) must approve the Founder Share Issuance — the direct grant of WTI Class A shares to Wen Han. The Charter Amendment itself automatically reclassifies all outstanding WTI common stock (including TopCo's shares) as Class B on a one-for-one basis; no separate TopCo consent to the reclassification is required. TopCo's preferred shareholders should note that their indirect WTI exposure shifts from Class A economic terms to Class B economic terms as a consequence of the universal reclassification.

The approval is documented as a certified copy of the relevant board resolution(s) of TopCo (the "TopCo Approval") and must be delivered to HCIC in form and substance reasonably acceptable to HCIC, no later than 30 days before the anticipated Closing Date and, in any event, prior to the filing of the Registration Statement on Form S-4 with the SEC.

Majority Preferred Directors. Under the statuten, the Majority Preferred Holders (holders of more than 60% of the combined voting power of all Preferred Shares — Series A-1, A-2, B-1, B-2, and C-1) are entitled to appoint a specified number of directors to the TopCo board. Their affirmative vote on the board is required by Article 13 for the listing approval — including item (iv) above. Identifying the current Majority Preferred Directors and confirming the approval process with Belgian counsel is the critical-path action item, particularly for item (iv) given its impact on TopCo's WTI voting rights.

TopCo is a party to the BCA for purposes of Section 4.13 (TopCo Board Approval) and Section 5.02(h). Wen Han is a party to the BCA for purposes of the Founder Share Issuance (Section 2.01(b), Section 4.12(a)) and will sign a Founder Share Subscription Agreement (Exhibit [●]).

Actions Not Required in v0.13

Because TopCo remains alive and does not dissolve — and because the Founder Share Issuance is a new issuance by WTI (not a transfer of TopCo's assets) — the following actions are not required:

Removed — No Longer Required
  • Investor Consent from Majority Preferred Holders (Art. 27) — no TopCo dissolution is occurring
  • Waiver of "Deemed Liquidation Event" treatment (Art. 34) — the Transactions do not constitute a liquidation event; TopCo preferred shareholders retain all rights under the statuten
  • Waiver of liquidation preference waterfall — no liquidation; preferred shareholders keep their preferences in TopCo
  • Preferred Share Conversion (Art. 10(iii)) — TopCo preferred shares are not being converted; they remain in TopCo
  • Extraordinary General Meeting (EGM) — dissolution vote not required (Arts. 24, 27, 36)
  • Liquidator appointment and in-kind distribution authorisation (Art. 33)
  • Belgian Official Gazette publication and creditor protection period
  • KBO / BCE deregistration — TopCo does not cease to exist
  • Exhibit F (Form of TopCo Investor Consent) — removed from Transaction Documents

No Deemed Liquidation Event — reclassification is not a transfer. Article 34 is not triggered. The Founder Share Issuance is a new share issuance by WTI — TopCo does not transfer or surrender any of its WTI shares. The reclassification of TopCo's WTI shares from common stock to Company Class B Common Stock is a change in voting class only; TopCo's economic value and WTI share count are unchanged. No assets leave TopCo; preferred shareholders retain all liquidation preferences, anti-dilution rights, and consent rights. Belgian counsel must confirm this analysis prior to the TopCo board meeting.

3
Drag-Along Rights — Automatic Termination Art. 11 statuten

Article 11 of the statuten provides that the drag-along rights set out therein terminate automatically upon the completion of a Qualifying IPO — defined as an initial public offering of any Group Company on a recognised securities exchange. The WTI NYSE listing is such a Qualifying IPO.

The consequences are:

  1. aDrag-along rights cannot be exercised by Wen Han (as the founding Class A shareholder of TopCo) to compel preferred shareholders to participate in the Transactions. This mechanism is therefore not available as a tool to ensure participation.
  2. bDrag-along rights terminate at Closing (the Effective Time). After the WTI NYSE listing is complete, no shareholder of TopCo can use the drag-along mechanism to compel other shareholders to sell or transfer their TopCo shares.
  3. cNo drag-along is needed in v0.13. Because TopCo is not dissolving and its shareholders are not receiving WTI shares in exchange for their TopCo shares, there is no "drag-along scenario" to navigate. Preferred shareholders remain shareholders of TopCo; they do not need to consent to any transfer of their own shares.

Wen Han's voting position. As the sole holder of Class A shares (3 votes each), Wen Han controls approximately 41.9% of TopCo's total voting power on a fully diluted basis. This is below the 50% threshold required to exercise drag-along rights under Article 11, which is an additional reason why the drag-along is not available as a mechanism to compel preferred holders — even if it had not terminated upon the Qualifying IPO.

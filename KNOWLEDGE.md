## 8. Owner's Manual — Vehicle Operation

### 8.1 Safety & Compliance

- This is a **battery-electric tractor unit** — operators must read the full manual before first use.
- **Speed limit 19 mph** applies when: entering non-motorized vehicle lanes, at railroad crossings, on sharp bends/narrow roads/bridges, making U-turns, descending steep grades, visibility under 164 ft (fog/rain/snow/dust/hail), icy/snow/muddy roads, or towing a disabled vehicle.
- **Never coast downhill in neutral** — this removes drive motor braking and risks loss of control. Also causes insufficient E-axle lubrication.
- Replace any seat belt subjected to collision impact.
- US safety defects: NHTSA 1-888-327-4236 or www.nhtsa.gov
- **Warranty is voided by**: unauthorized modifications to high-voltage systems, electrical systems, braking, steering, or TBOX; OBD tampering; non-approved parts or fluids; failure to service at Windrose authorized centers.
- Vehicle scrapping and HV battery recycling: Windrose or authorized recyclers only.
- Data security: Windrose collects vehicle location, driving behavior, audio/video per applicable laws.

### 8.2 Eco Driving
- Maintain steady speed; avoid harsh acceleration/braking.
- Never coast in neutral (E-axle lubrication risk).
- Use regenerative braking; plan braking well ahead.

### 8.3 Winter Driving
- Use coolant with freezing point below local minimum temperature.
- Keep battery fully charged before cold weather.
- Use snow chains or snow tires on snow/ice.
- Avoid sudden acceleration/braking/steering inputs.
- Increase following distance significantly.
- Snow chains: fit outer tires of drive axle both sides; remove on snow-free roads; do not exceed snow chain speed rating.

### 8.4 Hill / Ramp Driving
- If vehicle rolls back: depress brake immediately, apply EPB.
- On long descents: use regenerative braking, avoid continuous service brake (brake fade risk).
- Avoid sharp steering inputs on downhill roads (rollover risk).

### 8.10 Power, Brakes & Gear

#### Power ON / OFF
- **ON**: unlock vehicle → open door → swipe NFC Card on right instrument panel.
- **OFF**: EPB must be engaged; swipe NFC Card on right instrument panel, or Control Center > Setting > Power OFF. Power off only in **N gear**.

#### Service Brake
- Deceleration: release accelerator first, then brake if needed.
- Full stop: gradually depress brake, shift to N, apply EPB.
- Emergency: depress brake pedal forcefully.
- On long descents: use regenerative braking to avoid brake fade; do not brake continuously.

#### Electronic Parking Brake (EPB)
- Manual release: depress brake pedal, then press EPB switch.
- Auto-releases on startup in D gear (not R).
- Engage: pull EPB switch up.
- **Temporary parking**: press Temporary Parking Switch, or hold brake pedal 3+ seconds.
- **EPB emergency (service brake fails)**: pull EPB switch slowly using spot-brake technique; system applies full park brake when speed reaches 3 mph.
- **Towing mode EPB release**: hold EPB switch + tap Power OFF on screen + hold 5+ seconds → EPB stays released after power-off.
- **Emergency EPB release**: power on and press EPB switch 5 times within 10 seconds.

#### Gear Shift
- **R**: vehicle stationary, brake depressed, lever pushed up.
- **N from R**: lever pushed down 1 gear, hold 1 second.
- **D**: vehicle stationary, brake depressed, lever pushed down.
- Must power off in **N gear** only.

#### Regenerative Braking (KERS)
- Left KERS paddle: decreases intensity. Right KERS paddle: increases intensity.
- Levels 0–5 shown lower right of instrument cluster. Level 0 = off.
- Active only when battery SOC below 95%.
- Not a substitute for service braking; avoid in rain/snow/slippery conditions.

#### Driving Modes
- **ECO**: lowest energy demand; auto-engages below 10% SOC.
- **Normal**: balanced power and economy for daily use.
- **Power**: maximum output torque and fastest motor response.

#### Differential Lock
- Engage via Control Center > Setting > Bevel Differential.
- Only for slippery/muddy roads or heavy load climbing; do not use above 3 mph; do not turn while locked; release immediately after poor road conditions.

### 8.12 ADAS Systems

#### AVM — Around View Monitor
- Cameras stitch 2D/3D **270° panoramic view**.
- Access: Home > AVM, or auto-activates in R gear.
- Parking assist: distance-based visual/audible warnings during reversing.

#### MOIS — Moving Off Information System
- Monitors pedestrians/cyclists in front blind spots at low speeds.
- Stationary: visual alarm only. Moving forward: visual + audible.
- Auto-disables below 15 lux. Control: Control Center > ADAS > MOIS.

#### ACC — Adaptive Cruise Control
- Maintains set speed or follows preceding vehicle at set following distance (5 levels; default level 3).
- Scroll wheel up/down adjusts speed; scroll wheel left/right adjusts following distance.
- **ACC exits immediately if**: door opens, seat belt unfastened, not in D gear, brake depressed, EPB applied, ESC off, AEB activates, or radar/camera blocked.
- Best for dry highways; not recommended for city streets.

#### LCC — Lane Centering Control
- Press LCC button on steering wheel to activate lateral lane-centering.
- Keeps vehicle centered within lane markings at highway speeds.
- **Requires ACC active** and clear visible lane lines.
- LCC status indicator on instrument cluster. Deactivate via LCC button or steering wheel input.

#### AEB — Automatic Emergency Braking
- 4 warning levels: **safe distance** (green) → **Level 1** (yellow, low-frequency buzzer) → **Level 2** (red, high-frequency buzzer) → **automatic braking**.
- Can detect: pedestrians, cyclists, passenger cars, trucks in same lane.
- **Cannot detect**: oncoming/crossing vehicles, animals, traffic lights, walls.
- ESC/EBS must be functional for AEB to work. On by default.
- Control: Control Center > ADAS > AEBS.

#### EBS — Electronic Braking System
- Upgraded ABS/ESC; reduces brake response and pressure build time.
- Integrates ASR, HSA, ESC. Yellow EBS lamp on cluster = fault → contact service.

#### EBD — Electronic Brakeforce Distribution
- Regulates front/rear braking force for different load conditions.
- Controls rear wheel slip for stability.

#### ASR — Acceleration Slip Regulation
- Brakes slipping drive wheels during acceleration/startup.
- Deactivated when ESC is turned off.

#### LDW — Lane Departure Warning
- Operates at **37–75 mph**; visual and audible alert when vehicle drifts without intentional steering.
- Enabled by default. Control: Control Center > ADAS > LDW.
- Ineffective on roads without clear lane lines, in poor visibility, with snow chains, or with worn tires.

#### ESC — Electronic Stability Control
- Brakes individual wheels to correct oversteer/understeer; reduces motor torque to prevent rollover.
- On by default. Turning off ESC also disables ASR.

#### BSIS — Blind Spot Information System
- Detects pedestrians and cyclists in side blind spots.
- **Level 1** (yellow): cyclist 3–12 mph enters detection area → yellow warning lamp, optical signal.
- **Level 2** (red + audible): vehicle in D gear with turn indicator active on target side → red warning + audible alarm.
- Control: Control Center > ADAS > BSIS Alarm Sound.

#### ADDW — Driver Distraction Warning
- Warns when driver is distracted. Can be disabled for current power-on period.

#### ISA — Intelligent Speed Assistance
- Front camera + offline maps detect speed limit signs within 164 ft / up to 23 ft height.
- Flashes and sounds alarm when speed exceeds detected limit.
- Release accelerator or brake to cancel audible warning. Full off: Control Center > ADAS > ISA.

#### HSA — Hill Start Assist
- Prevents rollback on slopes; holds vehicle briefly after brake release.
- Driver must start within 3 seconds. Off by default at startup.
- Control: Control Center > Setting > Hill Start. HSA indicator flashes before deactivation.

#### TESD — Tire Emergency Safety Device
- Passive device on front wheels; prevents hub-to-ground contact during blowout.
- Remove before tire replacement; reinstall with new bolts/nuts only.

#### EHPS — Electro-Hydraulic Power Steering
- Speed-dependent assist (more at low speed, less at high speed).
- Active steering alignment after turns; hands-off detection (HOD).
- Contact service center immediately on fault.

#### ECAS — Electronically Controlled Air Suspension
- Remote control adjusts front/rear/lift axles independently; memory heights M1 and M2.
- Screen options: High (+1.97 in) / Standard / Low (−1.38 in from standard).
- Max raise: 3.35 in front/rear. Max lower: 1.97 in.
- Manual button disabled above 6 mph; auto-returns to standard above 6 mph.

#### TPMS — Tire Pressure Monitoring System
- Real-time tire pressure and temperature: Control Center > Monitoring.
- Warning lamp + popup + audible alert for: abnormal pressure/temperature, sensor loss, rapid leak.

#### Low Speed Alarm
- "ding-ding-ding" at low forward speed. "beep-beep-beep" in reverse.

### 8.13 Emergency Procedures

#### Towing / Rescue
- Install tow hook in front left or right mounting holes (screw fully in, no shaking).
- Keep vehicle powered for steering and braking.
- Turn on hazard lamps. Max tow speed: **9 mph** (to prevent motor damage).
- Disconnect trailer before towing. Do not tow laterally in mud or soft dirt.

#### Brake Failure
- Activate hazard lamps; hold steering wheel firmly.
- Slowly apply EPB using **spot-brake technique** (do not lock all at once).
- Use runaway truck lane if available.
- Do not immediately apply emergency brakes (sideslip/rollover risk).

#### Steering Failure — Straight Road
- Release accelerator; activate hazards; apply brakes evenly. Do not emergency brake immediately.

#### Steering Failure — Curves / Mountains
- Release accelerator immediately; apply brakes quickly.

#### Tire Burst
- Hold steering wheel firmly; activate hazards; release accelerator.
- Repeatedly and lightly depress brake; maintain straight line.
- Pull over safely when speed is reduced.

#### Vehicle Sideslip
- Release accelerator; turn wheel slightly toward slipping side and return.
- If sideslip caused by braking: release brake immediately.
- Do not brake and steer simultaneously. Stabilize first, then apply intermittent braking.

#### Accidental HV Power-Off While Driving
- Press hazard switch; try to restart.
- If unsuccessful: pull over using inertia force.

#### Emergency Evacuation (doors cannot open)
- Pull emergency ring behind cover above left net bag → breaks left window glass.
- Connect sleeper protection net to left side wall fixed point → use as escape rope to exit.

#### Vehicle Fire
- Small fires: dry powder / CO₂ / dry sand extinguisher.
- Large fires or deformed/leaking battery: **large continuous volumes of water**; move combustibles away.
- Inform emergency services: BEV with HV battery — electric shock, chemical, and thermal runaway risks.

#### Wading Rescue
- Vehicle may have hidden HV damage even without visible external damage.
- Full PPE required. Allow vehicle to dry completely before any further work.

#### Rescue Personnel PPE Requirements
- Class 0 insulating gloves (1000V rated)
- Impact-rated safety goggles
- Insulated tools rated for high voltage
- Insulating rescue hooks
- Lithium-battery-rated fire extinguisher
- **Buddy system mandatory** — one supervising while other works
- Remove all metal jewelry before rescue operations

#### High-Voltage Cutoff — From Outside
1. Park; apply EPB.
2. Pull hood release handle twice.
3. Remove low-voltage MSD from engine compartment.

#### High-Voltage Cutoff — From Inside
1. Park; apply EPB.
2. Open lower-left instrument panel trim.
3. Disconnect low-voltage MSD.

*In a vehicle collision, the HV system deactivates automatically.*

#### High-Voltage System Locations
- Battery: under vehicle floor
- Right: CCS1 charging port
- Left: MCS charging port
- Orange high-voltage harness
- Drive motor

### 8.14 Charging

- Charging ports on **both sides** of vehicle.
- Unlock via Control Center > Outside > Charging Port Unlock.
- **Minimum charger voltage: 1000V** — lower voltage devices cannot charge.
- Insert charging plug until "click" confirms; electronic lock activates when charging starts.

#### Charging Indicators (top of windshield)
| SOC | Indicators |
|---|---|
| < 30% | Left flashes |
| 30–90% | Left on solid |
| 90–100% | Left + middle solid; right flashing |
| 100% | All three on solid |

- SOC below **20%**: low SOC alarm.
- SOC below **5%**: power limit mode, speed reduction.
- Best operating temperature: **50–86°F**. Charge at least once every 2 weeks if unused.
- **Emergency charging port unlock**: push unlocking mechanism from parallel to perpendicular to vehicle forward direction.

### 8.15 Maintenance

#### Pre-Drive Checklist
- Connections and fastening; motor and E-axle noise; accessories; E-axle and steering tank oil levels; washer fluid and coolant levels; lubrication points; brake and steering function; electrical equipment; tire pressure; driver tools.

#### SOC Calibration (monthly and after 3+ weeks storage)
1. Discharge to 20–25% SOC.
2. Charge fully to 100%.
3. Discharge to 40–60% SOC.

#### Coolant
- Check level between MIN and MAX marks.
- Replace immediately if suspended matter, sediment, or discoloration.
- Check freezing point regularly.
- Filling: add slowly to MAX; run water pump 10 min with vehicle powered on; power off; top to MAX; tighten cap.
- Motor and room temperature must be ≤ 113°F during filling.
- Rinse system minimum 3 times when first adding or fully draining.
- **Never mix different coolant types.**

#### Tire Maintenance
- Replace after **6 years** of service.
- Wait for tires to cool before inflation/deflation.
- Check tread depth, foreign objects, cracks, dents, shoulder wear.

#### Tire Inflation Pressures
| Size | Pressure |
|---|---|
| 295/80R22.5 | 130.5 psi (all positions) |
| 315/70R22.5 | 130.5 psi |
| 12R22.5 | 134.9 psi |

#### Steering System
- Check free travel and looseness before each drive; shake steering wheel to confirm lock.
- Heavy steering, shaking, or offset requires **immediate service**.

#### High-Voltage Safety Maintenance
- Only trained and qualified personnel may perform HV work.
- Remove all metal jewelry; use insulated tools.
- Disconnect low-voltage MSD before any HV component work.
- **Buddy system mandatory.**

#### Low-Voltage Battery
- Located in engine compartment.
- Keep terminals clean and dry; apply acid-proof grease to clamp lower ends.
- Battery electrolyte is corrosive — rinse eyes/skin immediately with water.

#### Lighting Self-Test
- Control Center > Monitoring > Driving Light Self-Test — illuminates all lamps simultaneously.

#### Service Mode Switches (inspection/filling only — keep closed during normal driving)
- Speed limit test · motor coolant pump · battery coolant pump · left/right battery pack compressor · active grille shutter

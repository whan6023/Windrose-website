## Definition

PR (Public Relations) / communications support refers to the services and efforts provided to manage and improve the public image of an organisation, individual, or brand. It involves crafting and disseminating messages to stakeholders, the media, and the public to ensure positive perception and effective communication.

Key aspects of PR/communications support include:

1. **Media Relations** — introductions to, and relationship management with, Tier 1 media (see *Resourcing and Time Allocation* below).
2. **Branding and Messaging** — helping to shape and maintain consistent messaging that aligns with the organisation's values and goals.

PR/communications support helps an organisation establish strong relationships with both the public and internal stakeholders, fostering trust and transparency.

The Services under this agreement relate primarily to strategic communications, media relations and related advisory support.

Unless otherwise agreed in writing, the following activities are **not** included within the scope of this agreement:

- Event management or event logistics
- Event PR support, including guest list management or speaker coordination
- Influencer marketing or influencer engagement campaigns

Should the Client require support in these areas, Bold Voodoo may provide such services under a separate scope of work or additional fee arrangement.

## Consideration — Investor Introductions (Success Fee)

Separately from the option-based consideration for PR/communications work above, the Contractor will be compensated for **investor introductions** on a success-fee basis as follows:

1. **Success fee.** Where the Contractor introduces a prospective investor to the Client and that introduction results in a closed deal under which the Client actually receives investment monies, the Client shall pay the Contractor a fee equal to **2% of the Net Proceeds received** by the Client from that investor.

2. **Trigger — money received.** The fee is earned and payable only on completion of the relevant transaction and **only to the extent funds are actually received** by the Client. No fee is payable on commitments, term sheets, or amounts pledged but not received. Where funds are received in tranches, the fee is payable pro rata on each tranche as and when received.

3. **Net Proceeds.** "Net Proceeds" means the gross investment monies actually received by the Client from the introduced investor, less _[define deductions — e.g. any amounts repayable, and any third-party placement/broker/legal/professional fees directly attributable to that investment]_. _[Confirm whether 2% applies to equity investment only, or also to debt/convertible/grant funding.]_

4. **Attribution.** An investor is "introduced by the Contractor" where the Contractor is the **effective cause** of the Client first being put in contact with that investor. _[Recommend maintaining an agreed written list of introduced investors to avoid disputes; consider excluding investors already known to or in active discussion with the Client.]_

5. **Payment timing.** The success fee shall be invoiced on receipt of the relevant funds by the Client and paid within **14 days** of the Client's receipt of those funds (or of the relevant tranche).

6. **Tail period.** The success fee remains payable on deals with introduced investors that close within _[e.g. 12]_ months after termination of this Agreement, provided the introduction was made during the term.

7. **VAT.** The success fee is exclusive of VAT, which shall be added where properly chargeable.

8. **Regulatory.** The parties acknowledge that introducing investors and arranging investment deals for a fee may be a regulated activity and/or financial promotion under applicable law (including, in the UK, the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000). ## Data Protection

1. **Compliance with Data Protection Laws.** Both parties agree to comply with all applicable data protection laws, including the UK General Data Protection Regulation and the General Data Protection Regulation (EU) 2016/679 ("GDPR"), the Data Protection Act 2018 (as applicable), and any other relevant data protection or privacy legislation in force during the term of this Agreement.

2. **Definitions.** For the purposes of this Agreement:
   a. "Personal Data" means any information relating to an identified or identifiable natural person ("Data Subject") as defined under the applicable Data Protection Laws.
   b. "Data Controller" means the entity that determines the purposes and means of processing Personal Data.
   c. "Data Processor" means the entity that processes Personal Data on behalf of the Data Controller.

3. **Roles and Responsibilities.**
   a. The **Client** (Windrose) shall be the Data Controller with respect to any Personal Data provided for the purpose of this Agreement.
   b. The **Contractor** (Bold Voodoo Ltd) shall be the Data Processor and shall process Personal Data only on the documented instructions of the Client and for the sole purpose of performing its obligations under this Agreement.
   c. Both parties shall ensure that they implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure the security of Personal Data and to protect it from accidental or unlawful destruction, loss, alteration, unauthorised disclosure, or access.

4. **Data Processing Details.**
   a. **Purpose of Processing:** performing the PR / communications and media-introduction Services under this Agreement, including managing media and stakeholder relationships.
   b. **Types of Personal Data:** names, job titles, employers, and business contact details (e.g. email addresses and telephone numbers) of journalists, media representatives, Client personnel, and other business contacts.
   c. **Data Subjects:** Client personnel; journalists and media representatives; and other business and stakeholder contacts.
   d. **Duration of Processing:** the Personal Data shall only be processed for the duration of this Agreement, and shall be deleted or returned to the Data Controller upon termination of this Agreement, unless otherwise required by law.

5. **Data Subject Rights.** Both parties agree to cooperate in responding to any data subject requests, including but not limited to requests for access, correction, erasure, or restriction of processing of Personal Data, in accordance with applicable data protection laws. The Contractor shall promptly notify the Client of any request it receives from a Data Subject and shall not respond to such requests without the prior written consent of the Client.

6. **Sub-Processors.** The Contractor shall not engage any sub-processors to process Personal Data without the prior written consent of the Client. If consent is granted, the Contractor shall ensure that any sub-processor complies with the same data protection obligations set forth in this Agreement, and that a written contract is in place between the Contractor and the sub-processor that includes data protection provisions consistent with the terms of this Agreement.

7. **Security Measures.** Both parties shall implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to ensure a level of security appropriate to the risk, including encryption, access controls, and regular audits of the security measures in place to protect Personal Data.

8. **Data Breach Notification.** In the event of a Personal Data breach, the party discovering the breach shall notify the other party without undue delay, but in any event within 72 hours after becoming aware of the breach. Both parties shall cooperate to assess the breach, mitigate any damages, and notify the relevant supervisory authority and Data Subjects, where necessary, in accordance with applicable data protection laws.

9. **Audits and Inspections.** The Client may, upon reasonable notice and during normal business hours, audit the Contractor to ensure compliance with the terms of this Data Protection provision and any applicable data protection laws. The Contractor agrees to provide the Client with reasonable access to records and facilities to conduct such audits.

10. **Indemnification.** The Contractor shall indemnify and hold the Client harmless from any claims, losses, or damages arising from the Contractor's breach of its obligations under this Data Protection provision, including any fines or penalties imposed by a supervisory authority.

11. **Termination.** Upon termination of this Agreement, the Contractor shall, at the option of the Client, return or delete all Personal Data in its possession, except to the extent retention is required by law. The Contractor shall provide the Client with written confirmation that all Personal Data has been returned or deleted.
